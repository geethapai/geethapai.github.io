Citizens Connect Redesign

As a final apprenticeship challenge, Erica Horowitz, Hamy Pham and I were asked to redesign and build a prototype of Boston’s Citizens Connect website in 2 weeks. Citizens Connect is a portal on the City of Boston’s website that enables people to learn more information about city services, report an issue or request a service. Unfortunately the current website is a giant mess of links under various broad categories – it’s essentially a site map of everything on the website (see above).

Process

All three of us would be involved in the entire process, but each of us would “own” a portion of the work. While all of us would work on strategy, I would own the wireframes, prototyping and user testing, Hamy would develop the visual design and Erica would execute the front-end code.

Information architecture:Our first step was to make sense of the current site. We got together in light-filled conference rooms and started pulling apart each section of Citizens Connect. Right away, it appeared to be a convoluted mess. We noticed that each of the larger sections, like Business, included multiple types of information, like making a business complaint and information on licensing and permits.

UX Redesign: EITC Website

User Personas & Wireframes

top
top

Overview

For several years I served as a volunteer IRS tax preparer for the Boston’s Earned Income Tax Credit Coalition. The Earned Income Tax Credit is a federal credit that is eligible to people who make below a certain income level. Millions of dollars go unclaimed every year. The coalition ensures that many people who are low-income take advantage of the credit by providing free tax preparation services. In this case study, I reimagined Boston’s EITC website.

Problem

The Boston EITC Coalition’s website contained a great deal of information, but it have been could be better organized so users could find the information they need more easily. The current site’s main page contained a lot of information about resources that are not related to the EITC, which made it difficult to navigate. In addition, important information was scattered and difficult to read. While the city attempted to organize information by user type within a side navigation menu, there was also information on the main page that pertained to taxpayers, which could be found in the the taxpayer section. In addition, there were multiple links on the main page that pertained to the same information.

Process

  • Analyzed usability and IA issues on Boston EITC website
  • Developed personas and user goals based my years of field experience in 2 different tax prep locations
  • Developed wireframes based on persona goals

Result

After thinking through the personas groups and their goals, I knew that it would be important to speak to each of those groups from the home page. My goal was also to clean up the design and make text more legible and easier to read. Furthermore, I wanted to make sure information would be easy to find – so I would use large buttons with information targeted to my four personas. The four main categories would be:

  • Taxpayer Info: This page contains Earned Income Tax Credit eligibility requirements and a list of materials that taxpayers should bring to tax sites.
  • Free Tax Prep section: Simple page with a search function and a list of the tax prep sites in Boston.
  • Additional Resources: Contains other financial resources that do not directly pertain to EITC but are important services.
  • Volunteer Info: The EITC volunteer program is run by another Boston-area nonprofit, so this link would take the user directly to that site.
